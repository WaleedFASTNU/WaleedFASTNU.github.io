Digital Identity Management System Through Blockchain

About the Project

In today’s architecture of sharing credentials and information with another party, everything is centralized which makes it prone to forgery, impersonation, damage, theft along with many other things [1]. In traditional online banking systems, the sender and receiver of a transaction achieve their goals due to the presence of a middle party in between which is the bank itself. This communication occurs on the basis of trust as both the sender and the receiver trust the bank to process the transaction properly. What if the sender and receiver were to achieve their respective goals without any intermediate roleplayer so that their communication stays solely between them? This is what a blockchain offers. The decentralized nature of blockchain provides users a unified, interoperable and tamper-proof infrastructure enabling secure management and storage of information without the need for a middle entity. The information regarding an individual’s identification stored on the internet is known as digital identity. Using the blockchain technology, this digital identity can be secured in the form of Non-Fungible Tokens in the name of the rightful owners who can choose where their data goes, who they want to share it with. The primary motive of this project is to provide a self-sovereign identity [2] management system for securing the authenticity of an individual’s identification and make its attestation process easier by the use of a blockchain. The verifier of any digital identity can verify its validity by looking at the issuer's (government, university etc) signature on the blockchain after they request access from the holder (owner of the digital identity). In traditional systems, the centralized authority holds the user's account and hence their digital identity. As a result, this third party can control the account, disable it or revoke the user's access at any time. However, through the use of Blockchain infrastructure and Decentralized Identifiers [3], we aim to put the control of their digital identities back into the hands of the users.
